Learn about Stacks




A Stack is a data structure that is frequently used in computer science, it is commonly described as a Last in, First out data structure.

Stacks can be used to store data, and LIFO refers to that the data in the stack can only be accessed in the reverse order it was added in. A helpful way to think about stacks is to compare them to how a pack of 3 tennis balls works. The container is cylindrical and just wide enough to fit a tennis ball. Only the top most tennis ball can be accessed and it is also the most recently added. Thus, the top most tennis ball is the "last one in" and must be the "first one out", just like in the image below.

It can also be helpful to think of the items of a stack organized vertically, with the top of the stack representing the last item added. There are 2 main methods to interact with stacks.

  • Push - Adds something to the top of the stack.
  • Pop - Removes something from the top of the stack.

How to play the stacks game:

  • The goal of the game below is to make the Output box match the Goal box. Both are listed on the right side of the Game window.
